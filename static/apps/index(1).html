Methodology & thresholds

Content and face validity: each expert rates every item on a 4-point scale (1 = not relevant/clear, 2 = somewhat, 3 = quite, 4 = highly). Ratings of 3 or 4 count as agreement. Content validity ratio: each expert rates every item as Essential, Useful but not essential, or Not necessary.

Item level (CVI / FVI)

I-CVI = (experts rating 3 or 4) / (total experts who rated the item)

Scale level (CVI / FVI)

S-CVI/Ave — mean of all I-CVIs. Acceptable at ≥0.90 (Polit & Beck, 2006; Polit, Beck & Owen, 2007).
S-CVI/UA — proportion of items with I-CVI = 1.00 (every expert agreed). Acceptable at ≥0.80.

Modified kappa (chance-corrected agreement)

Raw I-CVI doesn't adjust for the chance that experts agree by luck alone, especially with small panels. The modified kappa corrects for this:

Pc = [N! / (A!(N−A)!)] × 0.5^N  —  probability of chance agreement
κ = (I-CVI − Pc) / (1 − Pc)

where N = experts who rated the item, A = experts agreeing (rating 3 or 4).

Interpretation (Cicchetti & Sparrow, 1981; Fleiss, 1981): κ > 0.74 excellent · 0.60–0.74 good · 0.40–0.59 fair · <0.40 poor.

Face Validity Index (FVI)

Identical formulas applied to expert (or target-population) ratings of item clarity and comprehensibility rather than relevance — reported as I-FVI, S-FVI/Ave, and S-FVI/UA.

Content Validity Ratio (CVR)

Lawshe's method asks each expert to rate every item as Essential, Useful but not essential, or Not necessary.

CVR = (ne − N/2) / (N/2)

where ne = number of experts rating the item Essential, N = total experts who rated the item. Each item's CVR is compared against a minimum (critical) value determined by panel size:

CVR ≥ critical value → item retained as Essential. 0 < CVR < critical value → item needs revision. CVR ≤ 0 → item should be rejected. The scale-level CVR (Overall) is the mean of all item CVRs.
